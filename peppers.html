Capsicum

Hot and spicy, life and death

Installation, 2016-2018

Capsicum are better known as peppers. But let's be real, the cool type of pepper is really the chili pepper (üå∂Ô∏è). In commercial settings and in frost-prone areas they are usually grown as annuals. However, if you care for them, they will live longer.

Plants, unlike, animals are rarely anthropormized. Why not? With pets you can have surrogate babies. Now you can have surrogate offspring in plants, too.

Early months

Chili babies are growing in an incubator. They need lots of light. It's a race to the top. Not everyone will get a spot in the coveted pot, so speed and health are key for success. If they could chirp like birds, we would say the squeaky wheel gets the grease. Of course, we love them all equally.

Teenage months

The youngin are ready to go from indoor warmth to harsh outdoors. It's May, so not that harsh. The recommendations say to bring them inside at night. We do a daily trip with them for a couple of weeks. That's it, they are now "hardened".

Mature months

Fully grown, lush green, and ready to produce. Sometimes it doesn't rain, so we bring them water. When will the magic happen? Literally out of air they make many, many peppers. We are so proud.

Old age

As winter comes, we trim them and wait for the next spring to come. Eventually, after another full season, it's time for a new generation. Maybe they could have gone longer, but it's best to end on a high note.
